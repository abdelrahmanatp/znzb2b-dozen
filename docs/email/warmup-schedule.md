## Overview

This schedule ramps a brand-new sending domain from cold to warm over 6 weeks. Starting at 20 emails/day and gradually increasing to full campaign volume ensures high deliverability and protects long-term domain reputation.

**Campaign volume target:** 522 prospects × 3 email sequences = ~1,566 total sends (across 6 weeks = ~265 emails/day average at full ramp)

**Segment targeting strategy:** Start with the **boutique hotels & lodges** segment (45% of 522 = ~235 prospects) because this mid-tier market typically has higher engagement rates (20-25% open, 3-5% reply), which strengthens domain reputation before moving to more difficult segments.

## Monitoring Thresholds & Escalation Protocol

### Bounce Rate (Hard Thresholds)

| Threshold | Action | Escalation |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| <1.5% | Healthy. Continue warmup as scheduled. | None |
| 1.5–3% | **Warning.** Pause volume increase. Hold at current day's volume for 2 more days before next ramp. Investigate top bounce reasons (invalid domain? typo list?). | Alireza (cold-email specialist) investigates; logs finding to docs/email/bounce-log.md |
| 3–5% | **Action required.** Stop increasing volume. Stay at current level for 3–5 days. Run email validation on remaining list. May need to re-scrub prospects (Google maps, duplicates, typos). | Escalate to Abbie; pause outreach by 1 day if needed. |
| >5% | **CRITICAL — Immediate pause.** Stop all sends immediately. Investigate root cause: (a) list quality (duplicates, typos, old data), (b) sending domain reputation (check Google Postmaster Tools, SendGrid reputation dashboard), (c) email provider issue. Do not resume without Abbie approval. | **Escalate to Abbie immediately.** Per project rules (§ Project-Specific Operating Rules, #6), bounce rate >5% triggers automatic pause and escalation. |

### Open Rate (Health Check)

| Threshold | Action |
|-----------|--------|

| Threshold | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| <0.1% | Healthy. Continue. |

## Campaign Structure (3 Email Sequences)

Once warmup is complete, the campaign will consist of 3 sequences across the 522 prospects:

- **Sequence 1 (Day 1):** Initial outreach — value prop, product mention, link to catalog
- **Sequence 2 (Day 5–7, if no reply):** Follow-up — social proof (logo wall), specific product benefit
- **Sequence 3 (Day 14–21, if no reply):** Final push — limited-time offer or urgency (seasonal discount, new product alert)

Each prospect receives 1 email per sequence, never more than 1 email per week (deduplicate_leads.py enforces this).

## Decision Points & Branch Logic

### If bounce rate hits 3% on Day 4 (Week 1)
1. **Pause increase.** Stay at 20/day for Wed–Fri.
2. **Investigate:** Pull bounce report from SendGrid. Are bounces hard (invalid address) or soft (mailbox full, server down)?
3. **Action:**
   - If >50% are hard bounces: list quality issue. Validate email column in source list (Google Sheets) for typos, invalid domains. Fix and re-import.
   - If >50% are soft bounces: provider issue. May pass in 24–48 hours. Retry soft bounces once.
4. **Resume:** Day 6 (Mon) at 20/day again. If bounce rate drops, continue; if not, escalate to Abbie.

### If bounce rate hits 5% anytime
- **STOP ALL SENDS immediately.** Do not send another batch.
- **Escalate to Abbie with:** (a) bounce report, (b) sample of bounced addresses, (c) root cause hypothesis.
- **Hold:** Wait for Abbie approval before resuming (could be 1–2 days of investigation).
- Per project CLAUDE.md (§ Project-Specific Operating Rules, #6), this is non-negotiable.

### If open rate is <15% after Week 2 (boutique segment)
1. **A/B test subject line:** Run 2 variants of Sequence 2 to 50 prospects each (Week 3).
2. **Test cohort A:** Current subject ("Expert Hotel Linen Supplier — 30+ Clients Trust Dozen")
3. **Test cohort B:** Alternative ("We Supply TUI Blue & LUX* — Your Hotel Deserves Better Linens")
4. **Measure:** Which gets higher open rate? Deploy winner to remaining cohort Week 4.
5. **Update:** Adjust copy playbook for Sequence 3 based on winner.

### If one segment (e.g., Villas) shows bounce >4% while others are <1%
1. **Pause that segment.** Continue boutique + luxury warmup.
2. **Investigate:** Are villa prospects from a different source (lower quality scrape)? Are emails formatted differently?
3. **Clean:** Run email validation (tools: Clearout, NeverBounce, or Sendgrid's built-in validation).
4. **Resume:** Restart villas segment at 10/day (lower than before) once cleaned.
5. **Monitor:** If bounce stays >2%, remove segment from campaign; focus on boutique + luxury for Phase 1.

## Contingency: If Warmup Fails

If after Week 4 bounce rate remains >3% despite all corrective actions:

1. **Pivot to list quality:** Use email validation service (Clearout, ZeroBounce) to filter to highest-confidence addresses only.
2. **Consider shorter warmup:** If cleaned list is <300 addresses (good quality), may launch with 2-week warmup instead of 6.
3. **Consider domain change:** If outreach.dozensupplies.com is permanently damaged (>5% bounce sustained), may need to switch to new subdomain (outreach2.dozensupplies.com) and restart warmup.
4. **Escalate:** Abbie decides whether to pivot to cleaned list, use new domain, or pause campaign.
