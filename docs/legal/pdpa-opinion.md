# PDPA Lawful Basis Opinion — Dozen Hotel Supplies
**Prepared by:** legal-advisor
**Date:** 2026-05-08
**Status:** DELIVERED
**Jurisdiction:** United Republic of Tanzania
**Governing law:** Personal Data Protection Act No. 11 of 2022 (PDPA); Personal Data Protection (Personal Data Collection and Processing) Regulations, GN No. 449C of 2023 (PDP Regulations)
**Enforcer:** Personal Data Protection Commission (PDPC)

---

## Executive Summary

Dozen Hotel Supplies has a viable lawful basis to conduct cold B2B email outreach to 522 hotel and hospitality prospects in Tanzania under the PDPA 2022, subject to mandatory conditions that must be satisfied before any email is sent. The strongest available basis is **legitimate interests** — Dozen's commercial interest in reaching relevant business buyers is proportionate and not overridden by the data subjects' privacy expectations, given the strictly B2B nature of the contact, the professional context of the data, and the relevance of the product offering to the recipients' business functions. This basis is reinforced by the fact that the data was collected from publicly accessible sources and that every outreach email will include a conspicuous, frictionless opt-out. However, the PDPA does not provide a clean B2B carve-out equivalent to the GDPR's "soft opt-in" or the UK ICO's business email guidance — professional emails are technically personal data under Tanzanian law, and direct marketing requires careful handling. The verdict is **GO WITH CONDITIONS**: eight specific conditions must be met before the first email batch is dispatched. Most critically, Dozen must complete PDPC registration (if not already done — the April 30, 2025 deadline has passed and enforcement is active), execute a Data Transfer Agreement with SendGrid and Google, and ensure every outreach email contains the mandatory disclosure elements set out in Section 4 of this opinion.

## 1. Lawful Basis Analysis

### 1.1 The Three Candidate Bases

The PDPA 2022 recognises the following grounds for lawful processing (drawn from Section 25 and the Collection and Processing Regulations):

| Basis | Applicable here? | Assessment |
|-------|-----------------|------------|
| Consent | No | Consent must be "freely given, specific, informed and unambiguous" and obtained prior to collection. Dozen has no pre-existing relationship with the 522 prospects and has not obtained prior consent. Seeking consent before outreach would require contacting the prospects — which is the activity requiring a lawful basis in the first place. Consent is circular and unavailable. |
| Contract performance | No | No contract exists or is being performed with these prospects. Pre-contractual measures apply only where the data subject initiated the pre-contractual relationship. These prospects have not done so. |
| Legal obligation | No | No Tanzanian law obligates Dozen to contact these prospects. |
| Vital interests | No | Not applicable to commercial outreach. |
| Public task | No | Dozen is a private commercial entity. |
| **Legitimate interests** | **Yes** | Available where processing is necessary for the legitimate interests of the data controller and those interests are not overridden by the data subject's rights and interests. Analysis follows. |

### 1.2 Legitimate Interests Analysis

The PDPA's legitimate interests basis (consistent with Section 25 read with the Processing Regulations) requires a three-part assessment:

**Step 1 — Is there a genuine legitimate interest?**

Yes. Dozen has a clear, real commercial interest in marketing its products to businesses that foreseeably need those products. Hotel supplies (towels, bed linen, bathrobes, F&B linen, kitchen and sanitation goods) are operational necessities for every hotel, resort, lodge, and villa on the prospect list. The interest is specific (not general marketing to random persons), relevant (hospitality procurement is the recipients' job function), and lawful (B2B sales is a standard legitimate commercial activity).

**Step 2 — Is processing necessary for that interest?**

Yes. Email is a standard, proportionate channel for B2B outreach. The only alternative — phone cold-calling — is more intrusive, not less. The data processed (company name, contact person name, professional title, professional email, phone) is the minimum required to send a relevant, personalised business introduction. No sensitive personal data (health, financial, biometric, criminal record) is involved.

**Step 3 — Do the data subjects' interests or rights override Dozen's interest?**

No — on balance, they do not, for the following reasons:

- The data subjects are **professionals acting in a business capacity** (hotel procurement managers, general managers, operations directors). They are contacted in their professional role, not as private individuals. The PDPA does not explicitly create a B2B carve-out (see Section 2 below), but professional context is a material factor in the balancing exercise that reduces the weight of privacy expectations.
- The data was collected from **publicly available commercial sources** (Google Maps, web directories). Publication in a business context carries an implicit expectation that business contact will follow. A procurement manager whose professional email appears on a hotel's website or Google Maps listing has reduced expectation of privacy for that specific data point in the context of relevant B2B contact.
- The outreach is **directly relevant to the recipient's operational role**. This is not spam. Hotel procurement managers are the precise audience for hotel supplies. The connection is self-evident and non-intrusive.
- Every email will include a **clear, frictionless opt-out** (see Section 4). The PDPA grants data subjects an absolute right to object to processing for commercial purposes. Honouring that right immediately upon request eliminates ongoing harm.
- The volume is modest (522 prospects) and the communication is one-to-one and personalised, not mass broadcast.

**Conclusion:** Legitimate interests is the correct and strongest available lawful basis for this outreach. Consent is not required and is not obtainable in this context without making the entire outreach paradoxical.

### 1.3 Important Caveat on Direct Marketing

The PDPA 2022 and its Regulations impose an additional layer on top of the lawful basis: data subjects have an **absolute right to opt out of any processing of personal data for commercial advertising** without requiring justification (Section confirmed by FPF analysis). This does not negate the legitimate interests basis — it means that the opt-out mechanism is not merely good practice but a substantive legal entitlement that must be honoured immediately and without friction. Dozen's unsubscribe mechanism must therefore be functional before the first email is sent, and opt-outs must be actioned within 48 hours.

## 2. B2B vs B2C Treatment Under Tanzania PDPA 2022

### 2.1 Statutory Position

The PDPA 2022 does not draw an explicit distinction between B2B and B2C personal data. The definition of "personal data" covers any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person. A hotel procurement manager is a natural person. Their name, professional email address, job title, and phone number are personal data under the Act — regardless of the business context in which that data was collected or used.

There is no exemption in the PDPA comparable to the UK's concept of "corporate subscribers" (which excludes sole traders and partnerships from some PECR provisions) or the GDPR soft opt-in for existing business customers. Tanzania's law applies uniformly.

### 2.2 Professional Email as Personal Data

A professional email address of the form `john.smith@baraza.co.tz` is personal data. It identifies a natural person. The fact that it is provided in a professional capacity does not strip it of that character under Tanzanian law.

A role-based address of the form `procurement@baraza.co.tz` presents a closer question — it identifies a role rather than a named individual and may not constitute personal data where the role-holder is not identifiable from the address alone. However, where Dozen's prospect file links that address to a named contact person, the combination becomes personal data regardless of the address format.

**Practical consequence:** Dozen must treat all prospect data — including professional emails — as personal data subject to the full PDPA regime. No "business contact" shortcut applies.

### 2.3 Mitigating Factor

While the law does not formally create a B2B tier, the professional context is directly relevant to the legitimate interests balancing exercise (see Section 1.2 above). It reduces the weight of the data subject's privacy expectation without eliminating it. The result is that legitimate interests is easier to establish for B2B outreach than for B2C outreach — but the formal legal obligations (registration, transparency, opt-out, data processing agreements) apply equally.

## 3. Data Collection Method

### 3.1 Publicly Available Source Data

The 522 prospect records were collected from publicly accessible commercial sources — Google Maps, web directories — in 2024 and 2025. The PDPA explicitly permits indirect collection of personal data where the data is publicly available (Collection and Processing Regulations, read with Section 25). This is a recognised exception to the general requirement of direct collection from the data subject.

**Effect on lawful basis:** The public-source origin of the data strengthens the legitimate interests analysis. Data voluntarily published in a commercial directory carries a lower privacy expectation in the context of relevant B2B contact than data obtained through private means. It does not, however, eliminate Dozen's obligations as data controller — the PDPA obligations attach at the point of collection, not at the point of first use.

### 3.2 Transparency Obligation at Collection

Where data is collected from a source other than the data subject, the PDPA requires the data controller to inform the data subject of the collection and its purpose at the time of first contact (or within a reasonable period). The cold email itself satisfies this obligation if it contains the mandatory disclosure elements set out in Section 4. This means the first outreach email is simultaneously the first point of transparency notice — it must therefore contain identity and purpose disclosures in the email body, not merely in a linked privacy policy.

### 3.3 Data Currency

Prospect data collected in 2024 and 2025 may contain stale contact information. Sending to invalid addresses inflates bounce rates (the project threshold is 5%) and may result in messages reaching unintended recipients. Before outreach begins, Dozen should run the prospect list through an email validation tool (e.g., ZeroBounce, NeverBounce) to remove invalid addresses. This is both a compliance measure (ensuring data accuracy, a PDPA principle) and an operational deliverability measure.

### 3.4 No Re-Use for Other Purposes

Data collected for the purpose of hotel supplies outreach may not be repurposed for unrelated processing without a fresh lawful basis. If Dozen later wishes to use prospect data for a different purpose (e.g., market research for a new product category), a new basis analysis is required.

## 4. Required Email Elements (Compliance Checklist)

Every outreach email sent to the 522 prospects must include the following elements. These are mandatory under the PDPA 2022 (transparency and lawfulness principles, data subject rights) and under applicable anti-spam frameworks. Failure to include any of these elements makes the outreach non-compliant.

### 4.1 Mandatory Elements

| # | Element | Requirement | Implementation note |
|---|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Sender identity** | Full legal name of the data controller: "Dozen Hotel Supplies" | Must appear in the From field and in the email body |
| 2 | **Physical address** | Registered/operating address of Dozen | Include in email footer: "303 Silversand, Kibweni, P.O. Box 4212, Zanzibar, Tanzania" |
| 3 | **Purpose of contact** | Clear statement that the email is a commercial introduction and why the recipient was contacted | e.g., "We are reaching out because your property may benefit from our hotel linen and supplies range" |
| 4 | **Source of data** | Where Dozen obtained the recipient's contact information | e.g., "We found your contact details via [Google Maps / public hospitality directory]" |
| 5 | **Lawful basis statement** | Brief statement of the basis for processing | e.g., "We are contacting you on the basis of our legitimate business interest in reaching relevant hospitality buyers" |
| 6 | **Opt-out / unsubscribe mechanism** | Clear, functional, one-click unsubscribe | Must be prominently placed; must be honoured within 48 hours; must not require login or account creation |
| 7 | **Data rights notice** | Reference to data subject's rights: access, rectification, erasure, objection | Brief statement + link to privacy policy or contact email for rights requests |
| 8 | **Contact for data inquiries** | Email or address for privacy/data rights requests | Can be info@dozensupplies.com or a dedicated privacy@dozensupplies.com |
| 9 | **No deceptive subject line** | Subject line must not misrepresent the commercial nature of the email | CAN-SPAM and good practice: no "Re:" prefix or false urgency claims |
| 10 | **Pricing disclaimer** | All pricing references are indicative only and subject to confirmation | Required by Dozen's internal business rules; also manages legal exposure on misrepresentation |

### 4.2 Recommended Elements (Not Legally Required but Strongly Advised)

- Link to Dozen's full privacy policy on dozensupplies.com (establishes professional credibility and fulfils transparency best practice)
- Social proof reference (e.g., "trusted by TUI Blue, LUX*, Neptune Hotels") in the body — lawful, relevant, and reduces perceived intrusiveness of cold contact

### 4.3 Follow-Up Emails

All mandatory elements from 4.1 must be reproduced in every follow-up email in the sequence. A recipient who did not opt out after the first email has not consented to follow-up — the lawful basis re-applies on each send, and each email must independently satisfy transparency requirements.

## 5. Data Retention Rules

The PDPA 2022 does not prescribe fixed retention periods. The governing principle is that personal data must be retained only for as long as necessary for the purpose for which it was collected (purpose limitation and storage limitation principles). Dozen must define and document its own retention schedule. The following schedule is legally defensible and operationally practical:

| Scenario | Retention period | Rationale |
|----------|-----------------|-----------|
| Prospect — no response after full outreach sequence | 12 months from final email sent, then delete or anonymise | Processing purpose (attempted outreach) is exhausted; no ongoing legitimate interest justifies indefinite retention |
| Prospect — responded, no commercial relationship formed | 24 months from last interaction, then delete or anonymise | Residual legitimate interest in re-engagement within a reasonable business cycle; beyond 24 months the interest decays |
| Prospect — becomes a client | Duration of the commercial relationship + 5 years | Contract performance basis replaces legitimate interests; 5-year tail covers commercial dispute resolution and statutory limitation periods under Tanzanian law |
| Prospect — unsubscribed / opted out | Suppress immediately; retain suppression record indefinitely | Suppression list must be kept to prevent re-contacting an opted-out individual in future campaigns. Do not delete the record entirely — that causes re-contact. Retain only: email address + opt-out date + opt-out flag |
| Prospect — hard bounce (invalid address) | Delete immediately on bounce confirmation | No legitimate interest in retaining data for a non-deliverable address; continuing to store it creates data minimisation risk |

**Documentation requirement:** Dozen must maintain a written retention schedule and review it annually. This document constitutes the initial record of the retention policy for the outreach campaign.

## 6. Third-Party Processor Obligations

### 6.1 The Legal Position

Dozen is the **data controller** — it determines the purposes and means of processing the prospect data. SendGrid (email delivery) and Google (Google Sheets, used as the prospect database) are **data processors** — they process personal data on Dozen's behalf. Under the PDPA 2022, a data controller must not transfer personal data to a processor unless:

(a) The transfer is necessary for the controller's lawful purpose; and
(b) The processor provides sufficient guarantees to implement appropriate technical and organisational measures; and
(c) Processing by the processor is governed by a binding agreement (Data Processing/Transfer Agreement).

### 6.2 Cross-Border Transfer to the United States

Both SendGrid (Twilio) and Google are US companies. The United States has not been designated as a country with adequate data protection by the PDPC. Transfers to the US therefore fall under **Section 32 of the PDPA** (transfers to countries without adequate protection) and **Regulation 20 of the PDP Regulations**.

Under this framework:

**Option A — PDPC Permit (formal route):** Dozen submits an application to the PDPC disclosing: (i) the identity of the processor, (ii) the type of data, (iii) the transfer purpose, (iv) security measures in place, (v) evidence of a contractual agreement with the processor. The PDPC issues a transfer permit. This is the technically correct route under the letter of the law.

**Option B — Data Transfer Agreement (practical route):** A contractual agreement between Dozen and each US processor that binds the processor to PDPA-equivalent safeguards. This satisfies the Section 32(4) condition that the transfer is authorised under a contractual agreement. This is the mechanism most commonly used in practice by Tanzania-based businesses using international cloud services.

**Recommended approach:** Execute Data Transfer Agreements (DTAs) with both SendGrid and Google before any prospect data is loaded or emails are sent. Both companies offer standard Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) that include GDPR-standard obligations; these provide a sound baseline but must be supplemented with a Tanzanian law addendum confirming PDPA compliance commitments. Simultaneously, file notification with the PDPC of the cross-border transfer arrangement — even if a formal permit is not strictly required where a DTA exists, proactive notification demonstrates good-faith compliance and reduces enforcement risk during the current active enforcement period.

### 6.3 Processor Contractual Requirements

Each Data Transfer Agreement with SendGrid and Google must include at minimum:

| Clause | Required content |
|--------|-----------------|
| Purpose limitation | Processor may only use prospect data for the specific purpose (email delivery / spreadsheet storage) instructed by Dozen |
| Sub-processor controls | Processor must disclose sub-processors and obtain Dozen's approval before adding new ones |
| Security obligations | Processor must maintain technical and organisational security measures appropriate to the risk |
| Data subject rights | Processor must assist Dozen in fulfilling data subject access, rectification, erasure, and objection requests |
| Breach notification | Processor must notify Dozen within 72 hours of any security breach affecting Dozen's data |
| Return/deletion | On termination, processor must return or delete all personal data |
| Audit rights | Dozen has the right to audit the processor's compliance |

**Practical note:** SendGrid's standard DPA (available at sendgrid.com/policies/dpa) and Google's standard DPA (available via Google Workspace) both contain substantially the above provisions as they were designed for GDPR compliance. The key addition required for Tanzania is explicit reference to the PDPA and the PDPC as the applicable supervisory authority.

### 6.4 WhatsApp / Meta (Phase 2)

The same cross-border transfer analysis applies to Meta Platforms (WhatsApp Business API) when Phase 2 launches. A separate DTA will be required at that stage. Meta's existing Business Terms of Service and DPA should be reviewed against PDPA requirements before WABA activation.

### 6.5 PDPC Registration of Dozen as Data Controller

**This is a blocking prerequisite.** The PDPC required all data controllers to register by April 30, 2025. That deadline has passed and enforcement (including fines and legal action in collaboration with law enforcement) commenced May 1, 2025. Dozen must:

1. Determine whether it has already registered (if registered, confirm certificate is current)
2. If not registered: register immediately as a data controller via Form No. 1 with the PDPC before any data processing activity commences
3. Registration fee for a company of Dozen's likely scale: TZS 200,000 (medium-scale) or TZS 1,000,000 (large-scale) depending on employee count and turnover
4. Registration is valid for 5 years

Operating without registration exposes Dozen to fines between TZS 100,000 and TZS 5,000,000,000 (up to approximately USD 1.9 million at current rates) and potential criminal liability for individuals. No outreach should proceed until registration is confirmed.

## 7. Verdict

### GO WITH CONDITIONS

The outreach to 522 hotel and hospitality prospects in Tanzania is **legally permissible** under Tanzania PDPA 2022 on the basis of **legitimate interests**, subject to the following conditions all being satisfied before the first email batch is sent.

### What This Opinion Does Not Cover

1. **CAN-SPAM Act (US):** Because Dozen uses a US-based ESP (SendGrid), US anti-spam law may apply to the technical infrastructure. The compliance-auditor's CAN-SPAM checklist addresses this separately.
2. **East African Community data frameworks:** No EAC-level data harmonisation instrument currently overrides the domestic PDPA.
3. **Future regulatory developments:** The PDPC is an active and expanding enforcement body. This opinion reflects the law and enforcement posture as of May 2026. Material developments (adequacy decisions, sector-specific guidance on B2B marketing) should be monitored quarterly.
4. **WhatsApp Phase 2:** Meta WABA compliance requires a separate legal review at the time of WABA activation, particularly regarding template message consent requirements.
5. **LinkedIn prospecting:** Separate terms-of-service analysis required for LinkedIn Sales Navigator or enrichment tool use.
